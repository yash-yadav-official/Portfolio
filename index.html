Martian Rover

Mechanical Design 2019 - 2023

The exploration and manipulation of unstructured and dynamic environments have long been a cornerstone of robotics research. Autonomous rovers capable of performing complex tasks are essential in scenarios ranging from space exploration to disaster recovery, where human intervention is limited or impossible. The goal of this project was to develop a rover system capable of completing three primary objectives:

  • Panel Operation and Feedback Observation: The rover needed to operate a variety of elements on a control panel, including toggling switches, measuring electrical parameters, setting controls, and interpreting visual feedback indicators.
  • Probe Placement and Retrieval: The rover had to identify designated locations from a provided map, retrieve probes from an onboard storage unit, and place them accurately in these locations, demonstrating spatial awareness and dexterous manipulation.
  • Autonomous Navigation: The rover needed to traverse complex environments semi-autonomously and, eventually, fully autonomously, gathering environmental data along its route to aid decision-making.

These capabilities are critical in situations like extraterrestrial exploration missions or hazardous environments where a high degree of precision, adaptability, and autonomy is required.

Methods

The development process for the rover encompassed several stages, including mechanical design, electronic integration, algorithm development, and testing.

Mechanical Design

  • Manipulator Arm: A six-degree-of-freedom robotic arm was designed and mounted on the rover to enable precise control over switches, dials, and probes. The end effector incorporated a gripper with adaptive feedback mechanisms for handling various object sizes and textures.
  • Chassis: The roverâ€™s chassis was built for ruggedness, ensuring stability on uneven terrain. It featured independently controlled wheels for differential drive and pivoting capability.
  • Probe Cache System: A storage and retrieval subsystem for probes was implemented, equipped with sliding racks and quick-release mechanisms.

Electrical and Sensor Integration

  • Control Panel Interaction: The rover was equipped with tactile sensors to determine switch positions and rotary encoder feedback to measure the precise movement of dials. Electrical parameter sensors (e.g., voltmeters) were integrated for real-time measurements.
  • Navigation Sensors: LIDAR, cameras, and ultrasonic sensors were combined to create a robust Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) system. GPS modules augmented outdoor navigation.
  • Indicator Observation: A high-resolution camera was used to read visual indicators such as LEDs or analog gauges, with a vision-based feedback recognition algorithm.

Software and Autonomy

  • Autonomous Navigation Algorithms: Pathfinding algorithms like A* and Dijkstraâ€™s were implemented for real-time decision-making. The roverâ€™s onboard processor used deep reinforcement learning to evolve navigation skills over time.
  • Panel Interaction Programming: Manipulator actions were programmed using inverse kinematics and pre-recorded sequences, with machine learning enabling adaptive control for unfamiliar tasks.
  • Probe Placement Logic: Designated locations were identified using computer vision and pre-mapped coordinates. Error-correction algorithms ensured precision during placement.

Testing and Validation

A simulated environment mimicking the final deployment scenario was developed to test the roverâ€™s performance. Iterative testing cycles focused on refining mechanical accuracy, improving autonomy algorithms, and validating sensor feedback systems.

Results

The rover successfully demonstrated the ability to complete all objectives in controlled and semi-structured environments. Key outcomes included:

  • Panel Operation: The rover accurately toggled switches, with a success rate of 97% in laboratory conditions. Measurement of electrical parameters was precise, with deviations within a 1% margin of error. Indicatorsâ€™ feedback was interpreted correctly 95% of the time, using vision-based algorithms.
  • Probe Placement: The subsystem retrieved and placed probes at designated locations with a 93% success rate. Minor placement errors were attributed to challenging terrain conditions, which were mitigated by iterative software updates.
  • Autonomous Navigation: The rover achieved semi-autonomous navigation with human oversight, handling obstacles and route optimization efficiently. Initial testing showed partial success in fully autonomous operation, with an 85% completion rate in traversing complex paths. Further refinements in sensor fusion and decision-making algorithms improved this metric during later trials.

Discussion and Conclusion

This project underscores the feasibility of deploying autonomous rovers for complex, multi-functional tasks in challenging environments. The integration of advanced robotics with adaptive machine learning techniques enabled the system to achieve high precision and autonomy, meeting the demands of practical deployment.

The success of this project holds significant implications for future applications. In space exploration, similar rovers could operate control panels on extraterrestrial habitats, gather scientific data, and assist in human missions. In hazardous industries, they could perform tasks in environments unsafe for human workers, such as nuclear reactors or disaster zones.

Future work will focus on enhancing the roverâ€™s full autonomy, improving manipulator dexterity, and optimizing computational efficiency to enable real-time decision-making. Collaboration with domain experts in AI and robotics will further push the boundaries of what such systems can achieve.

STEM Product Design Challenge

Educational Design 2022

This project was part of a National Design Level Challenge organized by Dassault SystÃ¨mes, focusing on creating a product that could inspire students aged 10 to 16 to develop an interest in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics) subjects. The challenge required not only designing and manufacturing a functional product but also strategizing its marketing to ensure widespread adoption and appeal. The project spanned six months and involved a team of three members, including me.

Introduction

The declining interest of students in STEM subjects in the critical age group of 10 to 16 years has been a growing concern worldwide. This project aimed to address this issue by creating an innovative, hands-on product that combines learning with engagement. The product was required to:

  • Appeal to the target age group through interactive and fun features.
  • Be easy to assemble and use by children without requiring external help.
  • Align with current market trends and address the specific needs of our target audience.
  • Incorporate STEM concepts in a practical and comprehensible way.

Additionally, we needed to focus on cost-effective design and manufacturing to ensure the product could reach a wide audience without becoming prohibitively expensive.

Methods

The project was executed in several phases, including market research, product ideation, prototyping, testing, and marketing strategy development.

Market Research

We conducted a detailed analysis of current market trends in STEM products, identifying gaps in existing offerings. Surveys and focus groups with students, parents, and educators provided critical insights into the preferences and challenges of our target audience. Key findings included:

  • Students preferred hands-on activities that felt like games rather than structured learning.
  • Parents valued affordability and ease of use, while educators sought products that reinforced STEM curriculum concepts.

Product Ideation and Design

Based on our research, we conceptualized a modular robotics kit named "STEM Explorer," designed to teach basic programming, mechanical assembly, and problem-solving skills. The design process included:

  • Modular Design: The kit included snap-fit components that required no tools, allowing easy assembly by children.
  • Interactive Features: The kit incorporated a programmable microcontroller with simple drag-and-drop coding through an app, enabling students to create robots that moved, sensed obstacles, or performed tasks.
  • Educational Integration: Each kit included activity guides linked to STEM concepts, ensuring students learned as they built.

Prototyping and Manufacturing

The prototype was developed using 3D printing for initial testing. Iterations were made to improve durability, reduce complexity, and enhance aesthetics. The final design was manufactured using injection molding for cost-effectiveness, ensuring scalability for mass production.

Marketing and Engagement

To market the product, we developed a comprehensive strategy:

  • Collaborated with schools to organize workshops demonstrating the product's capabilities.
  • Created social media campaigns targeting parents and educators, emphasizing the product's educational value and ease of use.
  • Partnered with STEM advocacy organizations to distribute kits at discounted rates for underprivileged students.

Results

The project culminated in the successful design and demonstration of the "STEM Explorer" kit. Key achievements included:

  • Positive Reception: Feedback from students and educators during workshops indicated a high level of engagement and interest. Over 85% of participants expressed increased curiosity about STEM topics after using the product.
  • Ease of Use: Usability tests showed that students could assemble the kit and complete activities with minimal assistance, achieving a success rate of 92% in assembling the basic robot models.
  • Affordability: The final product was priced competitively, with a manufacturing cost that was 30% lower than comparable market alternatives.

Discussion and Conclusion

The STEM Explorer project demonstrated how thoughtfully designed educational tools can inspire young minds to engage with STEM subjects. By focusing on modularity, interactivity, and affordability, the product successfully bridged the gap between learning and fun.

This project highlighted the importance of aligning product design with user needs and market trends. It also underscored the value of integrating education with play to foster curiosity and creativity among students. The marketing strategies ensured that the product reached its intended audience effectively.

Moving forward, the kit could be enhanced with additional modules covering advanced concepts such as sensors, AI, and renewable energy systems. Expanding distribution channels and incorporating gamification elements in the coding app would further boost engagement and adoption.
